Hero Media

One standard for every hero slide — video and stills. Full-bleed, object-fit: cover, dark gradient overlay.

Must fit above the foldThe entire hero — headline, sub-line, CTA and brand row — fits within a single viewport (100vh) with no scroll, on desktop and mobile. Crop / design hero media so nothing critical is clipped at the edges.

Mobile

Rules that apply when the layout switches to its mobile form.

Breakpoint — mobile layout engages at max-width: 900px (the single source of truth; a few components fine-tune at 600px). Section vertical rhythm relaxes to --section-py: 96px (from 140px) at max-width: 960px.

Side padding — 20px floor, identical on every section & the footer. Content may sit further in, never less. Background layers and full-bleed imagery stay edge-to-edge. (See Layout.)

Type — every role keeps a fixed mobile px size (the mob column in Type Ramp) — no fluid scaling. Heading-stack rhythm (16 / 24 / 8) is unchanged.

Navigation — the desktop nav collapses to a hamburger that opens a full-screen overlay. Menu links use the Display role (48px). Hero phone / address tags and slide pagination are hidden.

Hero — the entire hero fits above the fold (one viewport, no scroll). Brand names render as stacked, uppercase text links. Media is 9:16 · 1080 × 1920. (See Hero Media.)

CTAs — full-width, stacked, label centered.

Carousels — inventory shows one card at a time with left / right arrows + dots; the “recently sold” slider steps one card per tap.

News & Events — the rows become an accordion: tapping an article opens its photo + copy directly beneath the title (one open at a time). Rows carry 16px internal padding so the active dark fill never glues text to its edge.

Reveals — slide-in transforms are clipped (overflow-x: clip) so off-screen motion never creates horizontal page scroll.
